NYIT IEEE MA

NYIT IEEE MA is the IEEE student chapter at NYIT Manhattan campus. We are under IEEE Region 1 which is the home to IEEE organizational units in the north-eastern part of the United States.

We act as your conduit to give you a glimpse into why you chose to be an engineer. We do that by working on challenging projects and participating in conferences across the country. We don't ask for people to join to just show up, we want colleagues and people who will work with us.

IEEE

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) is the world's largest technical professional society which aims to promote the development and application of electrotechnology and allied sciences for the benefit of humanity.

It is designed to serve professionals involved in all aspects of the electrical, electronic, and computing fields and related areas of science and technology that underlie modern civilization.

2017 IEEE MA E-Board

Priyank Kashyap

President

"I want to improve IEEE at NYIT-MA by giving back to the students. Through projects and workshops run by the students, I want to get more people involved and help students enhance their learning experience. I want to work closely with the IEEE NY Section to facilitate speakers coming and talking to students about their professional career or research. I see all of the above as crucial to create a engineering and innovativve culture here at NYIT."

Matthew Warshaw

Internal Vice President

"There should be a well known presence of what IEEE is, its accomplishments, and current activities on campus. When I first joined the club, professors didn't know it existed. Now, we hold competitions that brings students across departments together, to give them a taste of working in the real world. Moving forward, we shall continue to bring more of the real world's ethics and practices into the club; so that our memebers can be better prepared for an engineering career."

McGeorge Jean Baptiste

External Vice President

"I want to improve IEEE by making our student run group more effective in impacting the lives of our future Engineers. Too many times college students have graduated with Engineering Degrees but are unaware of how the Engineering Field actually works or they lack real world applications of the knowledge they have. I want IEEE NYIT to be the group that helps illuminate the gray areas in minds of many Engineers."
